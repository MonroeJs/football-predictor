Who Has the Best Odds to Win the 2026 FIFA World Cup?

Spain is currently the favorite to win the World Cup with +450 odds. France follows in second with +460 odds, and then England with +650 odds. Brazil is next with +800 odds. Portugal will be another team to watch, with +1000 odds to win the title. The 2026 tournament will be hosted across Canada, the United States, and Mexico from June 11 to July 19, 2026. These odds will continue to adjust as qualification progresses and as we get closer to the 2026 FIFA World Cup finals.

How do World Cup Betting Odds Work?

The team with the best odds to win the World Cup can fluctuate a lot during the event. Looking at the odds table, whichever country has the highest number with a minus sign is the favorite at that moment. If there are no minus signs, then the team with the lowest number after a plus sign is the current favorite.

World Cup 2026 Group Stage and Match Betting Markets

FanDuel Sportsbook also offers World Cup group betting odds, including markets for group winners and teams to qualify from the group stage. Each group presents unique matchups and betting opportunities, allowing you to back teams to advance or predict how each group will play out. Group betting adds another layer of strategy beyond just picking the tournament winner.

Golden Boot Odds and Top Scoring Markets

The Golden Boot market is one of the most popular World Cup futures markets because it allows bettors to focus on player scoring instead of only team success. Star forwards such as Harry Kane, Lionel Messi, and Cristiano Ronaldo are the type of names that can draw interest in Golden Boot betting, while younger stars and in-form scorers can also move up the board as World Cup 2026 gets closer. A player’s role, form, penalty duty, and number of matches all play a big reason in how Golden Boot odds are priced.

Top scoring markets can also create additional betting opportunities throughout the World Cup. Bettors can explore team goalscorer markets, compare player prices across platforms, and watch how odds move after major international matches or club seasons in the Premier League, Champions League, Bundesliga, and other top competitions. Whether you are backing a favorite or looking for longer odds, these markets add another layer to World Cup betting.

Team Props and Stage of Elimination Betting Odds

Explore a variety of team prop bets, including stage of elimination odds for countries like the United States, England, Canada, and more. These markets allow you to predict how far a team will advance in the tournament, from the quarterfinals and semifinals to lifting the World Cup trophy. Team-specific betting options add more depth beyond traditional match and winner bets.

FIFA World Cup Betting FAQs


How do World Cup futures odds change before the tournament starts?

World Cup futures odds can move well before the tournament begins based on qualification results, injuries, squad changes, and betting activity across the market. As World Cup 2026 gets closer, bettors can watch how the odds shift for favorites like Spain, France, Argentina, and Brazil, along with other contenders and dark horse teams that build momentum.

How do World Cup group stage odds and draw markets work?

World Cup group stage betting includes markets such as group winners, teams to qualify, and match odds for each game. Bettors can also track draw prices in three-way soccer markets, which is important because a draw can have a big impact on group stage standings and qualification paths.

Can you bet on the Golden Boot at FanDuel Sportsbook?

Yes, FanDuel Sportsbook offers Golden Boot betting as part of its World Cup futures markets. This gives bettors the option to back top scorers such as Harry Kane, Lionel Messi, Cristiano Ronaldo, and other players who could lead the World Cup in scoring.

Which teams could be dark horses at World Cup 2026?

Dark horse picks for World Cup 2026 will depend on group draw, recent form, and overall squad quality. Teams such as Portugal, Morocco, Croatia, Belgium, Japan, and Norway could attract interest from bettors looking for value beyond the favorites, especially if they build momentum before June.
